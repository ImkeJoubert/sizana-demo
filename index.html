The Cost of Inaction by Disease

The disease burden CHWs face daily — and the evidence for acting earlier.

SA's quadruple burden of disease disproportionately hits communities CHWs serve. Each condition has a clear, costed case for earlier detection and community-level management.

🫀
Hypertension & Diabetes
Tshwane CHWs screen 57,000 people aged 18–44 for hypertension every month. 7.6m SA adults have hypertension — 46% unaware. CHW early detection is 4× more cost-effective than clinic-initiated care. (IDCS 2025/26 · Gaziano et al.)
🦠
HIV & TB
265,000 people on ART in Tshwane. CHWs trace avg 2,150 HIV defaulters/month. TB referral rate from CHW screening: 0.06% — a documented system failure the district dashboard flags in real time. (DHIS2 Tshwane 2025/26)

Child Health

Why the Current System Fails CHWs
📚
No standardised training or knowledge base
Each province interpreted WBOT differently. Prior to 2018 each NGO trained for its own programme. The 2018 policy expanded scope but left content to provinces. In 2025, CHWs in NHI pilot districts were still retrieving information from outdated printed materials. Murphy et al. 2021 · Van Vuuren et al. 2025
👁️
Supervision is informal and non-standardised
"Development and support processes were informal and often lacking" — guidance documents "do not cross-reference each other." PHC managers nominally supervise WBOTs but are overburdened and unprepared. Assegaai & Schneider, HRH 2019
📊
Community data is collected but invisible
"WBOT asks you questions regarding your needs, but only God knows for what, because I haven't seen anyone acting on the collected data." — community member, South Africa. No pathway from field to DHIS2. Malatji et al., PLOS Global Public Health 2023
⚖️
Labour precarity drives turnover and erodes care
January 2025: Labour Court ruled CHWs must be deemed permanent employees — confirming decades of neglect. Stipends of R4,800/month, year-to-year contracts, and no career progression drive attrition. PSI 2025 · LRS 2025
